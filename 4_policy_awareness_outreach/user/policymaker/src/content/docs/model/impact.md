	Impact is determined by the degree to which an structure or system is exposed to a hazard (e.g., proximity to a flood zone) and its vulnerability to the effects of that hazard (e.g., structural weakness).

- Impact is determined by the degree to which an structure or system is exposed to a hazard (e.g., proximity to a flood zone) and its vulnerability to the effects of that hazard (e.g., structural weakness).
- Realized: Actual disaster impacts, including:
- (projected): Anticipated consequences of hazard events.
- Direct: Physical damage, casualties, and economic losses.
- Indirect: Cascading effects such as supply chain disruptions or societal displacement.
